<p><p>Myeloproliferative disorders (MPDs) are characterized by the overproduction of blood cells. The market is primarily segmented into two types: Ph+ chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) and Ph- myeloproliferative neoplasms (MPNs). Ph+ CML is driven by the Philadelphia chromosome and is typically treated with tyrosine kinase inhibitors. In contrast, Ph- MPNs include conditions like essential thrombocythemia and polycythemia vera, often requiring different therapeutic approaches, including JAK inhibitors and other supportive therapies, to manage symptoms and complications.</p></p>
