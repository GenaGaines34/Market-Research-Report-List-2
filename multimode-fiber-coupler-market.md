<p><p>The multimode fiber coupler market features several types classified by their splitting ratios: 1×2, 1×4, 1×8, and 2×2. A 1×2 coupler splits one input signal into two outputs, while a 1×4 splits it into four, allowing for greater distribution. The 1×8 offers even wider output options for expanding networks. The 2×2 coupler connects two inputs to two outputs, enabling more complex interconnections. Each type serves different applications in telecommunications, data centers, and optical networks, depending on signal distribution needs.</p></p>
