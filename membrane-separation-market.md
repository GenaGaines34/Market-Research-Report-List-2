<p><p>The membrane separation market encompasses various technologies, each suited for specific applications. Reverse Osmosis (RO) effectively removes impurities by using a semi-permeable membrane under pressure. Ultrafiltration (UF) provides separation based on particle size, filtering out larger molecules while allowing smaller ones to pass through. Microfiltration (MF) captures even larger particles and microorganisms. Nanofiltration (NF) sits between RO and UF, ideal for removing divalent ions and small organic molecules. Other technologies include pressure-driven and electrically driven methods, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
