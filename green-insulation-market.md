<p><p>The green insulation market features various eco-friendly materials, each with distinct properties. Mineral wool, made from rock or slag, offers excellent fire resistance and sound absorption. Cellulose, derived from recycled paper, is sustainable and provides effective thermal performance. Fiberglass, composed of recycled glass, is lightweight and non-combustible. Hemp insulation utilizes natural fibers for superior breathability and moisture control. Cotton insulation, often made from recycled denim, is soft and sustainable. Other materials may include sheep's wool or sustainable composites, catering to diverse insulation needs.</p></p>
