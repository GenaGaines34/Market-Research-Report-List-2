<p><p>The biodegradable medical plastics market comprises various types, including PLA (Polylactic Acid), PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates), PBS (Polybutylene Succinate), PCL (Polycaprolactone), and PVA (Polyvinyl Alcohol). PLA is widely used for sutures and drug delivery due to its biocompatibility. PHA offers excellent biodegradability, making it suitable for various applications. PBS provides good thermal properties, while PCL is favored for slow-degrading implants. PVA is primarily used for hydrogel applications. Together, these materials enhance eco-friendly practices in the medical industry.</p></p>
