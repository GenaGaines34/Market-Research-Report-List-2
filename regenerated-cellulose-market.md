Viscose type fibers are derived from wood pulp, offering softness and breathability, making them popular in textiles. Lyocell fibers, produced through a more environmentally friendly process, are known for their strength and moisture-wicking properties. Highly oriented fibers, featuring enhanced molecular alignment, provide superior tensile strength and durability, making them suitable for specialized applications. Together, these categories cater to diverse industries, including fashion and home textiles.</p></p>
